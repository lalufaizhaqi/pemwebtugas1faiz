Soekarno AKA King of Karbit

Died:21 June 1970 (aged 69)

Soekarno (born Kusno Sosrodihardjo) was the first president of Indonesia, serving from 1945 to 1967. He was the leader of the Indonesian struggle for independence from the Dutch Empire and a prominent figure in the Non-Aligned Movement.

Early Life

Soekarno was born to a Javanese schoolteacher and his Balinese wife. He was originally named Kusno, but following a series of illnesses, his parents renamed him Sukarno after a figure in the Mahabharata.
He was educated at the Technische Hoogeschool te Bandoeng (now ITB)

Political Career

Soekarno rose to prominence as a leader of the nationalist movement. He spent years in Dutch custody, including exile in Flores and Bengkulu.

Proclamation of Independence

On 17 August 1945, Sukarno and Mohammad Hatta declared the independence of the Republic of Indonesia.

Guided Democracy

In the late 1950s, Sukarno established a system known as "Guided Democracy" to bring stability to the country.

The Bandung Conference

He hosted the 1955 Asian-African Conference, which led to the creation of the Non-Aligned Movement.

Legacy

To this day, he is remembered as Bung Karno, the "Great Leader of the Revolution."

Family Life

Soekarno was known for his charismatic personality and his many marriages, which were often subjects of political and social discussion.
